# OptiFinish

## Updated Information Architecture Strategy Report

Value Added Coating Solutions Pvt. Ltd.  
Updated on top of the April 2026 strategy report

---

## 1. Purpose of This Update

This document builds on the earlier OptiFinish IA and digital strategy report and updates the information architecture based on:

- the finalized public IA direction
- closer review of the current functional website
- review of the local Z-TAP website structure
- clarified product ownership and service grouping

This update does not replace the original report's CRM, forms, Zoho integration, lead handling, or digital-consolidation strategy. Those decisions remain intact unless explicitly revised here.

---

## 2. Core Strategic Correction

The biggest refinement is that the new website should not be structured as a generic corporate site with product listings.

It should be structured as a brand hub for a deep industrial portfolio.

The current OptiFinish website already contains broad product coverage, but the IA is fragmented, shallow, and inconsistent. The newer Z-TAP website shows a much stronger depth model: it presents a product through narrative sections such as demo, mechanism, fit, line context, results, and technical capability.

The revised IA combines:

- the breadth of the current OptiFinish business
- the depth model established by Z-TAP

Z-TAP should now be treated as:

- the strongest design reference
- the strongest product-depth reference
- the primary technical baseline for the future main-site build

---

## 3. Strategic Website Positioning

The website must present OptiFinish as a company with three clear strengths:

- OptiFinish-manufactured systems
- OptiFinish proprietary automation products
- authorised partner products and systems

It must also show that OptiFinish supports customers beyond product sales through service, maintenance, commissioning, spare parts, and future support systems.

The IA should make it immediately clear:

- what OptiFinish manufactures
- what OptiFinish has developed
- what OptiFinish supplies through partners
- what OptiFinish services after the sale

---

## 4. Finalized Top-Level Navigation

The finalized public navigation should be:

- Home
- Products
- Services
- Facility
- Our Work
- Resources
- About
- Contact

This is cleaner than the earlier draft because:

- `Products` becomes the primary portfolio map
- `Services` becomes the service and after-sales layer
- trust layers are separated cleanly
- overlap between products and services is reduced significantly

## 7. Why This IA Is Better

### 7.1 It fixes the core ambiguity

The earlier structure risked confusion between:

- products
- automation
- partners
- generic solutions

The revised IA removes most of that confusion by using clearer structural roles.

### 7.2 It reflects the real business

The old site and early IA drafts underrepresented OptiFinish's own manufactured product depth.

The revised IA makes room for:

- full plants
- ovens
- booths
- proprietary automation
- partner products
- catalogue-based sister business offerings

### 7.3 It allows Z-TAP-style depth where needed

The Z-TAP site demonstrates the right storytelling model for high-value industrial pages.

That model should now be applied to important product families across the site:

- hero and positioning
- what problem the product solves
- how it works
- where it fits in the line
- technical capability
- proof and results
- CTA

### 7.4 It separates product and service logic properly

`Services` now becomes a true after-sales and support layer instead of a duplicate product taxonomy.

---

## 8. Depth Model for Major Product Pages

The new website should not treat serious product families as shallow brochure stubs.

Major pages should follow a depth model inspired by Z-TAP:

- Hero
- Problem framing
- Product fit or variants
- How it works
- Line integration / process role
- Technical capabilities
- Proof / case studies / deployment context
- Downloads
- CTA

This level of depth is most important for:

- Z-TAP
- ZA01
- Powder Coating Plant (Complete Line)
- GEMA
- DURR
- booth systems where differentiation matters

This depth standard is directly informed by the Z-TAP product site and should be treated as the quality benchmark for the broader website.

---

## 9. Hub vs Detail Responsibility

The hub website should:

- explain the brand clearly
- map the portfolio
- route users into the right area
- establish trust
- capture leads

Detailed product destinations should:

- explain the product deeply
- answer technical and operational questions
- handle product-specific persuasion
- convert serious buyers

The website should therefore operate as a hub with depth, not as a flat corporate catalogue.

---

## 10. Notes on Current Website vs Future Direction

### Current website strengths

- broad portfolio already visible
- existing blog and troubleshooting material
- existing product intent already present

### Current website weaknesses

- incomplete or inconsistent navigation
- shallow product storytelling
- mixed ownership signals
- weak separation of manufactured vs distributed products
- gallery and downloads not structured strategically
- generic enquiry pathways

### Future direction

Retain the portfolio breadth, but apply the Z-TAP standard of depth and clarity.

Z-TAP should also be treated as the main branding and implementation reference for the new site ecosystem, not merely as one isolated product page.

## 12. Forms, CRM, and Lead Handling

The earlier report's direction remains intact and is not changed by this IA update.

The following points remain valid:

- website enquiry forms route into Zoho CRM
- exhibition lead capture routes into Zoho CRM
- careers submissions route into a separate CRM pipeline
- Zoho Campaigns remains the outbound newsletter and blog distribution layer
- lead source handling remains centralized
- CRM and lead-routing logic should be preserved during the IA and website rebuild

This IA revision changes the public structure, not the CRM strategy.

---

## 13. Backend / Admin Scope

The backend should remain broadly defined for now.

At this stage it is enough to describe it as a future internal layer supporting:

- employee login
- lead capture
- content publishing
- future AI-assisted blog generation and review workflow

Detailed backend IA is deferred and should not block public site architecture.

---

## 14. Final Recommendation

The new OptiFinish website should be built as a premium industrial portfolio hub that combines:

- the product breadth of the current business
- the narrative depth demonstrated by the Z-TAP website
- the trust architecture required for high-value B2B buying
- the lead and CRM structure already defined in the original strategy report

Operationally, Z-TAP should remain both:

- one of the core product destinations
- the main design and technical reference point for broader site implementation

The finalized IA in this update should be treated as the structural base for the next stages of planning, content modeling, and build execution.
