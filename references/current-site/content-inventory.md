#### Post: What is Powder Coating Process and Powder Coating Line
- **Slug:** `what-is-powder-coating-process-how-does-it-works-what-are-component-of-powder-coating-line`
- **Date:** 2022-05-24
- **Status:** Published
- **Full Content:**

  **What is Powder Coating**

  Powder coating is a high-level strategy for applying embellishing and defensive completion to a broad scope of materials and items. The powder used for the cycle is a combination of finely ground particles of color and gum. It is showered onto a surface to be covered. The powder particles have a charge and they stick to the electrically grounded surfaces until warmed and melded into a smooth covered in a stove. The outcome is a uniform, robust and top-caliber, appealing completion.

  **What is Pre-treatment for Powder Coating**

  Pre-treatment is the chemical preparation method of various types of metal and aluminum. It is used before applying a powder-coated surface finish. In all powder coating systems, appropriate surface pre-treatment is necessary to ensure long-lasting results.

  **Pretreatment System** — It comprises tanks or tubs containing synthetic compounds or water. In it, the articles are plunged to clean and set up the surface for powder covering. The size of the tanks or tubs relies upon the size and amount of articles to be covered.

  **Powder Spray Framework** — the powder covering machine or powder coating system. This splashes electrostatically charged powder into the shower stall where the articles covered are hanging. Electrostatic powers give a specific and steady covering quality.

  **Relieving Stove (Curing Oven)** — In a relieving stove, powder-covered articles are put for 10 to 15 minutes at the upgraded temperature for the powder to liquefy, polymerize, wire, and fix into the last completion.

  **Types of Powder Coating:**
  - Thermosets powder coatings (epoxy, hybrid, acrylic — includes cross-linkers, chemically cross-linked)
  - Thermoplastics powder coatings (re-meltable on heat)
  - UV treatable powder coatings (photopolymerisable; melt at 110–130°C then instantly cured by UV)

  **Powder Coating Market Applications:** Appliance industry (largest single segment — refrigerators, dryers, dishwashers, microwaves). Automotive (wheels, door handles, engine blocks). Lawn/garden equipment. Sporting products. Architecture (metallic roofs, curtain walls). Office furniture. Vehicle parts (rims, door handles, window handles).

#### Post: What are Powder Coating Process and Powder Coating Plant
- **Slug:** `what-are-powder-coating-process-and-powder-coating-plant`
- **Date:** 2023-02-02
- **Status:** Published
- **Tags:** conveyorised powder coating plant, powder coating plant, powder coating plant for almirah, powder coating plant price in delhi
- **Full Content:**

  Powder coating is a popular process for finishing metal products, providing a durable and long-lasting coating that can resist wear, fading, and corrosion.

  **The Powder Coating Process:**

  The metal product is first cleaned to remove dirt, grease, and other contaminants, then coated using an electrostatic process. The metal product is charged with an electric field, and the powder coating material is charged with an opposite polarity. The charged powder particles are attracted to the metal product and deposited onto the surface. The coated product is then cured in an oven, which fuses the powder into a smooth and durable finish.

  **Powder Coating Plant Equipment:**
  - Pretreatment equipment — degreasing, phosphating, rinsing
  - Powder coating booth — spray guns and application equipment
  - Recovery system — recovers excess powder for reuse, minimizing waste
  - Oven — conveyor-type curing oven
  - Quality control equipment — measures coating thickness, cure, adhesion, hardness

  **Benefits of Powder Coating:**
  - **Durability:** Resists wear, fading, and corrosion. Ideal for furniture, appliances, automotive parts.
  - **Environmental friendliness:** Dry process, minimal waste and emissions.
  - **Cost-effectiveness:** Highly efficient for high-volume runs; recovered powder can be reused.
  - **Versatility:** Wide range of colors and finishes, from matte to glossy, solid to metallic.

#### Post: Gema Powder Coating Gun
- **Slug:** `gema-powder-coating-gun-machine`
- **Date:** 2022-07-13
- **Status:** Published
- **Tags:** gema powder coating gun, gema powder coating gun price, powder coating gun, powder coating spray gun
- **Full Content:**

  The electrostatic Gema powder coating gun helps in the flow of powder in the coating process. When charges are produced, they are deposited on the substrate in the form of powder.

  **Types of Gema Powder Coating Gun:**
  - Manual powder coating gun — for lower production volumes
  - Automatic powder coating gun — for high-volume production processes

  **Manual Powder Coating Spray Gun:** Used for lower production. Productivity and nature of the Gema powder coating gun machine are critical for high production.

  **Automatic Gema Powder Coating Gun:** Sprays powder naturally and covers all regions in the booth. A photo sensor in automatic guns detects whether the item is ready for coating and tracks which parts are coated vs. remaining. Requires minimum energy to start the process.

  **Tribo Powder Coating Gun:** Charges epoxy powder when powder material is sand with an insulator inside the gun. Generates positively and negatively charged particles that migrate to the product surface, creating a thin layer of coating.

  **Corona Coating Gun:** Delivers high voltage and creates an electrostatic field with low ampere. Creates an electrostatic field between the terminal of powder and the product. Suited for large-scale industries requiring high-speed coating of large areas.

  **Flame Powder Coating Gun:** Used when it is impossible for the oven to perform its function. Produces heat and ignites gases; the heat is combined with powder material causing the powder to melt. Applied where a lower cost process is needed, though there are risks with this technique.

  The powder coating spray booth is an important part of the powder coating plant. It is a chamber or section that helps in the powder coating process. In the powder coating spray booth, the powder is scattered on the substrate with the aid of a powder coating gun.

  **Parts of the Booth:**
  - Powder coating spraying area — powder is sprinkled on the article
  - Powder coating recovery area — the article is recovered after spraying

  **Working:**
  All color pigments (powder) are completely sprayed on the article in the spraying section. Then the article faces the recovery area. After the powder recovery the article moves to the curing oven.

  **Exhaust Fan:**
  The powder coating spray booth has an exhaust fan. When the dry powder is sprayed, contaminated air is produced. The exhaust fan eliminates contaminated air. Channels/filters must be cleaned consistently to prevent blockage — a clogged filter means the exhaust fan cannot get air through it and ideal results will not be achieved.

  **Pressure Balance:**
  A makeup air fan compensates for extracted air to maintain air pressure balance. Running both fans simultaneously achieves the ideal gas pressure:
  - Exhaust only: negative pressure, external air and contaminants are pulled in
  - Makeup air only: positive pressure, air pushes towards the door

  **Filter Systems:**
  Used in automotive industries, painting car bodies, bus bodies, etc. When spray is scattered, electromagnetism (positive and negative charges) is produced. Environment must be clean and safe for best results.

  **Applications:**
  - Metallic roofs and curtain walls
  - Office materials (chairs, desks)
  - Vehicle materials (wheels, rims, door handles, window handles)

#### Post: [Untitled — Types of Powder Coating Finish]
- **Slug:** (none — draft)
- **Date:** 2022-05-19
- **Status:** Draft (never published)
- **Full Content:**

  Types of Powder Coating Finish — Three major categories:

  1. **Thermoplastics:** Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC), Polyolefin, Polyester
  2. **Thermosets:** Epoxy, Epoxy-Polyester Hybrid, Acrylic, Silicon-Based Powders
  3. **UV Curable Powder Coats**

  Detailed descriptions of each:

  - **Thermoplastics** — Synthetic materials that can be formed multiple times. Metal's surface is first heated; successive cooling hardens the coating. Thick and resilient; excellent corrosion protection and weathering resistance.
  - **PVC** — Available in electrostatic spray (up to 8 mil thick) and fluidized bed (8–25 mil thick). Requires liquid primer for complete adhesion. Good for insulation applications.
  - **Polyolefin** — Resistant to adverse environments; smooth surface and good adhesion. Used on lab equipment components. Good solvent resistance.
  - **Polyesters** — Long-term, durable solution; most commonly used. Great flexibility, impact resistance, chemical and mechanical resistance. Huge choice of colours, gloss levels, and special effects.
  - **Thermosets** — Chemically react during baking to form a polymer network resistant to coating breakdown. Do not re-melt when cooled after heat re-applied. More resistant to high temperature. Excellent aesthetic appearance.
  - **Epoxy** — Hard coating; very good adhesion, flexibility, and chemical resistance. Available in wide range of colours with smooth or textured finish. Not recommended for exposed, open-air environments due to UV chalking.
  - **Epoxy-Polyester Hybrid** — Best chemical and corrosion resistance of all powders. Excellent electrostatic spray characteristics, good transfer efficiency, good penetration.
  - **Acrylic** — Highest quality technology base. Excellent chemical resistance, superior exterior durability, outstanding smoothness. Ideal for vehicle applications. High cost; compatibility issues exist.
  - **Silicon-Based Powders** — Used over decades for high-performance liquid coating. Allows high-performance protective and decorative coatings. Provides high performance for synthetic resins, rubbers, paints, inks.
  - **UV Curable Powder Coat** — Faster curing cycles with lower temperature requirements. Melts at 110–130°C; instantly cured when exposed to UV light. Great scratch and chemical resistance, adhesion and bond strength.
  - **Matte Finish** — Non-reflective, dull, unpolished finish. Excellent for hiding surface imperfections. High pigment content — quick and easy to apply to large surfaces. Sensible to chips and scratches; requires extensive care.
